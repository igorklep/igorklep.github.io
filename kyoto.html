Kyoto 5 lectures (2024)

Free Noncommutative Real Algebraic Geometry and Analysis: Selected Topics

I. Positive Noncommutative Polynomials

Main topic of Day 1 was Helton's sum of squares theorem with the elementary convexity inspired proof. An alternate proof inspired by complete positivity is due to McCullough and can be found at https://doi.org/10.1016/S0024-3795(00)00285-8. It is Theorem 0.2, and the proof in Section 4 needs Section 2 on noncommutative Hankel matrices.

Board pictures of Day 1

Day 2 proved the Helton-McCullough archimedean Positivstellensatz, followed by an excursion into commutative real algebraic geometry. We discussed Putinar's Positivstellensatz and Hilbert's 17th problem. Lastly, we touched upon applications of noncommutative real algebraic geometry to quantum physics via the Navascues-Pironio-Acin (NPA) hierarchy and Bell inequalities.
